Project Overview

Zombie Survival is a browser-based co-op game for two players. One player hosts and shares their ID, the other joins with no server required. Together you defend a building from escalating zombie waves, managing barricades, ammo, and money while working toward a single goal: repair the car and escape.

The building has multiple rooms to unlock as you progress, each requiring money and coordination. A mystery box offers powerful weapons at random, a generator room powers a sliding security door, and a final garage holds the car that ends the run when fully repaired.

Key Features

  • Peer-to-peer Multiplayer: Two players connect directly via PeerJS with no backend. The host simulates zombies and syncs state to the joiner at 60Hz.
  • 8 Weapons: Pistol, shotgun, machine gun, UZI, desert eagle, ray gun, railgun, and blaster, each with unique stats, visuals, and behaviour. Most are available through the mystery box.
  • Barricade System: Windows around the building can be boarded up with planks. Zombies attack and break through to get inside.
  • Unlockable Rooms: Spend money to open new areas of the building including a side room, generator room, east wing, and final garage, each adding new strategic options.
  • Power Switch: Activating the generator opens a sliding security door and unlocks windows in the garage, letting zombies approach from a new direction.
  • Car Repair & Escape: Repair the car across five stages at £500 each, triggering a cinematic win sequence where the car drives out and the run ends.
  • Boss Waves: Every fifth wave spawns a boss zombie with significantly more health, a larger hitbox, and higher damage.
  • Turret: An automated turret in the east wing targets and fires at nearby zombies when powered on.

What I Learned

🌐

Peer-to-Peer Networking

Implemented a host/joiner architecture over WebRTC using PeerJS. The host owns the simulation and the joiner sends inputs, receives state, and handles visual-only effects locally to stay responsive.

🔄

State Synchronisation

Learned how to keep two clients consistent by syncing zombie positions, wave state, barricade health, room unlocks, and game events like kills and pickups without a central server.

🏗️

World & Collision Design

Built a rect-based wall system with circle-rectangle collision resolution. Rooms, doors, barricades, and furniture are all defined geometrically and the collision and rendering systems share the same data.
